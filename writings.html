A few Short Essays on the world as I understand it. Ideas I return to often, written the way I think.

I
On the fringe
Pay Attention to the Fringe

Do not ignore the fringe. The fringe is not always fringe; sometimes, they are only the fringe in your mind. For what is fringe today could become wisdom tomorrow, and we call it foresight. The fringe is always adopted later, after we become a little wiser. So, in hindsight, you were always wrong, and the fringe was right.

Wisdom is always hidden in the midst of other useless fringe notions.

From evolution to washing hands, doctors hello, personal computing, internet, blockchain, democracy, human rights, anti-segregation, anti-slavery. There are a lot of fringe notions that will never make sense, true, but amongst them, you will find some wisdom. A few wise notions that will give you an unbelievable edge.

So pay attention to the fringe, for what is fringe today will be wisdom tomorrow.

II
On optimism
Detail-Oriented Optimism

The uninformed optimist. The realistic optimist. The pessimist.

The uninformed optimist eventually becomes a pessimist, burdened by disappointments. The realistic optimist has a lot more success and achieves seemingly impossible feats, because he is armed by details.

He is not just directionally aware. He is aware in the right directions, and detail-aware.

He takes unconventional routes and bucks the trend, taking actions against the conventions of the day. He succeeds where the uninformed optimist fails, because he is armed by details, and his goals — though guided by inspiration — are built on the solid foundation of details. When he takes flight, he is not just taking flight of dreams. He is propelled by details, and his navigations directed by knowledge.

The uninformed optimist fails because he is solely guided by dreams and inspiration. Lacking in details, he flounders, missteps and stumbles, and usually fails. Falling into heartbreak and pessimism over time.

Do your best to be detail-oriented about your adventures. Acquire knowledge and study before your escapades. Understand your goals, and the details and the realities surrounding them. This is the path of realistic optimists. The actual winners, not those who merely got lucky.

They tend to have more wins, and fewer failures, allowing them to be optimistic and more efficient over a long time horizon. In essence, you become a very wise child — possessing the excitement of a child, with the mastery of experience.

III
On success and opportunity
Way of Our World

Success is rewarded with more opportunities for success, and failure is punished with the withdrawal and denial of opportunities to succeed.

He who has more is given more, allowed the opportunities to obtain more, and can easily obtain more. He who has little is given less — nothing at all, or even taken from — is rarely allowed the opportunities to obtain more, but rather is allowed the opportunities to obtain very little, lose more, and can hardly obtain more.

Think about this.

Can the kids who have all their needs met not study better than kids who show up to class on an empty stomach? A cycle that leads to a disparity in performance, up until college. Then after they graduate, they are given opportunities according to their performance in school.

The poor man will hardly feed healthily. He must sacrifice his own health because he has little. The rich or middle class can manage to feed healthily, hence they gain more for having more.

To he who has more, more will be given. To he who has less, more will be taken from.

Your duty, then, is to find a way to break this cycle.

IV
On greatness
Become the Figure

For some, their pedigree becomes their brand, and what their identity is beholden to. It is more important to be the brand yourself — your identity not tied to external factors, like what people would think or feel, or what institutions you are part of.

Be the brand yourself. Let your skills and results speak for themselves and everything will take care of itself. You will inevitably become a brand, and sometimes a better brand than those you wanted to be associated with.

Greatness need not associate itself with a brand to get legitimacy. If anything, it is greatness itself which gives such a brand legitimacy.

The hierarchy of prestige stands no chance against the hierarchy of greatness. Greatness outlasts institutions of prestige. Cultures transform, traditions are abandoned, institutions falter, but greatness is always in your history book — written, narrated, because it makes its dent.

Brand is a signal. Greatness is substance. Signal is subject to all manner of hackery and politics. Substance you can never fake. Substance without signal creates its own signal — free from the limitations of existing signals, it has the chance to be the better signal. Signal without substance is soon met with disgrace.

It must have been prestigious to paint for the monarchy. The monarchy itself, a brand that confers prestige. However, you do not remember the kings great artists painted for. But the artists and their work continue to influence our culture.

V
On technology and human experience
HCI in the Age of AI: The Most Consequential Field in Computing

For me, HCC/HCI is all about computers and how they should be able to shape the human experience. It is the design of computers, what they will do, what people will be able to do with them, how they should be able to shape their lives and interaction with the world, even when they are not using them.

The design of social media, for instance, has come to shape our attention span, thereby affecting our lives and how we interact with others, even when we are not on our phones. HCI determines what corporations should be able to do, and how they relate with users. The design of smartphones and social media has pulled back a curtain that once existed and given corporations access to our psychological profiles, tastes, and inner lives. That is not a technical achievement. That is a design decision.

In some instances, the UI and HCI designs of technology products are more consequential than the technology that powers them.

The nature and quality of the possible interactions with a product will determine the extent to which people can use it beneficially. This is especially true with hardware products. Get it wrong and it does not matter how sophisticated the underlying technology is.

Looking out at the world today, AI will bring about a lot of change in the next few decades. HCC/HCI is the only field that can accurately channel and shape what those changes will be. It is not just about usability. In the age of AI, it is about how these systems shape us, our outlook, our connections, our sense of who we are.

AI possesses the properties of an individual, without being one. It can build intimacy. During the internet era, it was a battle for eyeballs. With AI, it is a battle for intimacy and attachment. The field that shapes that battle is HCI.

VI
On investing
Social Investing

There are two types of investing. Financial and social.

Financial investing offers direct financial returns. Social investing offers social returns, and financial returns too, but indirectly, through a longer and more powerful chain of events.

Consider this: if in your community you have the option to offer scholarships to promising young people, or to invest in a fast-growing tech startup, the startup, at first glance, offers better returns. It is growing. The numbers are clear. The social investment is diffuse, hard to measure, slow.

But on the long run, the scholarship is sometimes the better investment.

A better-educated society is inspired to pay the favor forward. This creates a recursive loop. Wiser, more educated people engineer better policies. Better policies produce more functional economies. More functional economies produce more promising startups, more of them, better ones. You will, over time, be richer and live in a richer society. The scholarship funded the startup ecosystem you could not see yet.

This is one of the instances where investing for social returns prevails over the allure of immediate financial ones. The returns are real. They are just slower, larger, and shared with everyone around you.

VII
On prosperity
Building a Wealthy Society

Building a wealthy society comes down to two basic things.

Making the difficult necessary tasks easy. And making the scarce necessary commodities and services abundant.

All societies that struggle financially are failing at one or both of these. Nigeria, for instance. Canada, for instance.

Scarcity of what people need, and difficulty in obtaining it. These are the two roots of poverty. Everything else is downstream.

The government that understands this and acts on it systematically will produce prosperity. The government that does not, through incompetence or malevolence, will produce the opposite.

VIII
On progress
Sustainable Progress Is Engineered

Societies are systems. The better you understand the system, the better you will be able to tune it for the results you seek.

To have sustainable progress, not the type commonly found in African countries, where you take three steps forward and fifteen more backwards, you must have a systematic understanding of the society, almost to the point of pictorial. This way you can engineer the right laws and policies to get a continuous flow of the results you want. You will also know how to build resilient institutions to keep these in place and continually improve them.

Sustainable progress is engineered and never stumbled upon.

It takes a certain level of dumbness to be bloodthirsty. Beyond a certain level of intelligence, you cannot be. The societies that have understood this, that reserved the severest punishments for political violence and built institutions instead of factions, are the ones whose names appear in the history books as models.

Everything necessary for a prosperous society — from investor confidence, ease of business, availability of goods, education, research, to the invention of tools that make life easier — rises and falls on the policies and culture of a society.
